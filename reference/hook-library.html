The Hook Library

Fill-in-the-blank hook formulas, sorted by the psychological trigger they pull — each one rewritten for your message of oneness & unbreakable families. Keep this open when you draft. Steal shamelessly, then make it true.

The 4 triggers every hook pulls (pull at least one, stack two for power):
  • Curiosity gap — open a loop the brain needs to close.
  • Pattern interrupt — say/show something that breaks the expected.
  • Self-relevance — "this is about my life / my family."
  • Emotion / loss aversion — we feel a potential loss ~2× a gain.

1 · Curiosity-gap hooks

Trigger: open question · withheld answer. The brain hates an open loop.

FormulaYour-message example
The one ___ that ___"The one conversation that quietly breaks most marriages."
Nobody tells you ___"Nobody tells you the Bible has a blueprint for the home."
Here's what ___ won't say about ___"Here's what most churches won't say about family."
What if ___ wasn't ___?"What if a 'broken family' was never God's design — or yours?"

2 · Pattern-interrupt hooks

Trigger: violate the expected. Contradict common wisdom, then promise to explain.

FormulaYour-message example
___ is actually ___ — here's why"Love isn't the foundation of a family — oneness is. Here's why."
Stop ___. Do this instead."Stop trying to 'fix' your spouse. Do this instead."
You've read this verse wrong your whole life"You've probably read Genesis 2:24 wrong your whole life."
Unpopular: ___"Unpopular: a strong family isn't 50/50. It's 100/100."

3 · Self-relevance hooks

Trigger: name the exact person and their exact situation. Specificity = "that's me."

FormulaYour-message example
If you ___, this is for you"If you grew up in a home that felt divided, this is for you."
Parents of ___: ___"Parents of teenagers — you have less time than you think."
For anyone who ___ but still ___"For anyone who left church but still wants a whole family."
3 signs your ___ is ___"3 signs your family is starving for oneness without knowing it."

4 · Emotion / loss-aversion hooks

Trigger: a stake, a cost, a quiet fear. Negative framing gets priority attention — use it honestly, never manipulatively.

FormulaYour-message example
The cost of ___ is ___"The real cost of a 'busy' home is a child who feels unseen."
I almost ___ until ___"I almost gave up on my family — until one idea changed everything."
Your ___ remembers ___"Your kids won't remember the gifts. They'll remember the table."
Don't wait until ___ to ___"Don't wait until they're grown to build oneness at home."
Stacking (the pro move): the strongest hooks pull two triggers at once.
"Nobody tells you the Bible has a blueprint for the home — and you're already living against it." → curiosity gap + loss aversion.

The 5-point hook check

  • Under ~12 words / ~2.5 seconds to say aloud.
  • One trigger minimum (curiosity / interrupt / self-relevance / emotion).
  • Specific, not generic — "the dinner table," not "spending time together."
  • True to the clip — the payoff must actually deliver. No bait-and-switch.
  • Works on mute — say it AND put it on screen as text in the first 2 seconds (60%+ watch with sound off).

The ministry guardrail: a hook may create tension, but it must never create a false promise or fear you don't resolve. Invite, don't argue. Serve, don't sell.
