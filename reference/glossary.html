Reference · Shared Language

Glossary

The words we'll use the same way every lesson. If a term drifts, this page is the source of truth.

The craft

Hook
The first ~2–3 seconds of a video whose only job is to earn the next 3 seconds. Spoken line + on-screen text + opening visual, working together.
Hook Point
Brendan Kane's term: the single attention-grabbing idea that makes someone stop and think "tell me more."
Trigger
The psychological lever a hook pulls: curiosity gap, pattern interrupt, self-relevance, or emotion/loss-aversion.
Curiosity gap
An open loop — a question or claim with the answer withheld — that the brain feels compelled to close.
Pattern interrupt
Something unexpected (a claim, sound, or visual) that breaks the scroll's autopilot.
Loss aversion
We feel a potential loss roughly twice as strongly as an equal gain — why "what you'll lose" framing grabs attention.
Stacking
Combining two triggers in one hook for extra pull.

The numbers

3-second retention
The % of viewers still watching at 3 seconds. The gatekeeper metric: above ~65% and the platform shows your clip to far more people.
Hold / watch-time
How long viewers stay. Drives distribution more than likes or follows.
Vanity metric
A number that looks good (views, likes) but doesn't prove ministry impact.
Relationship metric
A number that reflects real connection: replies, returns, DMs, study attendance, families engaging.

The funnel

Reach / Retain / Minister
The three funnel stages: get found → keep people & own the relationship → walk with them toward unbreakable families.
Rented vs. owned audience
Rented = followers the platform controls. Owned = an email list / group chat you keep even if the algorithm turns. Moving people from rented to owned is the core retain move.
Depth ladder
The rungs from shallow to deep: clip → full sermon → podcast → website → owned list → online study → in-person.
Clip
A 20–40s standalone moment cut from longer teaching — the raw material a hook sits on top of.

The message

Oneness
The lens this ministry reads Scripture through; a distillation of Divine Principle, applied to building unbreakable families. (Definition to be refined by the pastor.)
Invite, don't argue
The posture: content opens a door and serves; it never debates or pressures.
