The Online Ministry Funnel

Reach → Retain → Minister. One page that shows how a stranger scrolling TikTok becomes part of your family. Every lesson in this workspace plugs into one box below.

Stranger scrolling→ Hook→ Watches clip→ Follows→ Deeper content→ Owned audience→ Online study→ In-person / discipleship
The one rule that governs the whole funnel: Invite, don't argue. Serve, don't sell. Every stage gives value first. The message — oneness, unbreakable families — is never bait-and-switched; the hook is true to the content it opens.

Stage 1 Reach — get found by strangers

Goal: earn the first 3 seconds of someone who has never heard of you.

  • The hook is everything here. Clips that hold >65% past 3 seconds get 4–7× more reach — the algorithm rewards the hold, not the follower count. A zero-follower clip can reach thousands.
  • Source of clips: mine your existing sermons, podcast, and in-person teachings for the single most compelling 20–40 second moment. You already have a vault.
  • Platform by audience: TikTok skews 16–30 (discovery engine); YouTube Shorts is best for 25+ and long-tail search; Reels is strong for DMs/community. Post the same clip to all three.
  • Volume + iteration beats perfection: test multiple hooks on the same clip; let 3-second retention pick the winner.

Taught in: Lesson 0001 (The 3-Second Hook), and the Hook Library.

Stage 2 Retain — turn viewers into a following you own

Goal: stop renting attention from the algorithm; start owning the relationship.

  • The ladder of depth: short clip → full sermon on YouTube → podcast → website → email / text list or group chat. Each post should point one rung deeper.
  • "Rented vs. owned": followers are rented (the platform can throttle you tomorrow). An email list, a group chat, a Discord — those you own. Moving people there is the single most important retention move.
  • Consistency signals safety: a predictable cadence (e.g. same theme each weekday) teaches the audience when to come back.
  • Reply as ministry: answering comments, praying for people by name in DMs — this is where "follower" becomes "person who feels known."

Taught in: Lesson 0002 (The Next Rung). Future: weekly cadence, the reply-as-ministry habit.

Stage 3 Minister — walk with people toward unbreakable families

Goal: real relationship — online study, then in-person / discipleship.

  • Online study as the bridge between anonymous scrolling and the Mobile group: a recurring live/Zoom/Discord gathering anyone, anywhere can join.
  • Invitation, not pressure: the path from viewer → study → in-person is offered repeatedly and gently, never as a hard ask.
  • Measure what matters: not just views — track conversations started, people who return, families engaging the tools. Vanity metrics feed Stage 1; relationship metrics define Stage 3.
  • Mobile is the prototype: what works in person there becomes the template you scale online, and online relationships can feed back into local groups elsewhere.

Taught in: Lesson 0003 (The Open Door). Future: the welcome script, the relationship scorecard, outcome metrics.

The engine that feeds all three stages: none of this works if the content runs dry. Lesson 0004 · The Sermon Mine turns one sermon into a week of clips, so a two-person team can sustain the whole funnel without burning out.

Where to start (your next 4 weeks)

  • Week 1: Master the hook (Lesson 0001). Ship 3 clips, 3 hooks each, pick winners.
  • Week 2: Establish a clip-from-sermon pipeline + a posting cadence.
  • Week 3: Add the first "owned audience" rung (a simple list or group chat link).
  • Week 4: Announce a recurring online gathering; start inviting from your content.
