Lesson 0002 · Retain

The Next Rung

One clear invitation per post — moving each viewer exactly one step deeper, toward a relationship you own instead of rent.

⏱ ~14 minutes🎯 One skill: write a single, low-friction CTA✅ Win: pick 1 owned home + 3 CTAs

A great hook with no next step is a leaky bucket. People feel something, then keep scrolling and forget you by lunch. The fix isn't more asking — it's one ask, pointing one rung deeper. The single most important version of that ask: move people from a platform you rent to a place you own.

Rented vs. owned — the whole game

🏚 Rented

Followers, views, the For You page. The platform decides who sees you — organic reach can be as low as ~5% of your own followers, and an account can be throttled overnight.

🏡 Owned

A text list, email list, or group chat. You reach these people directly. Church SMS opens at ~98%. No one can take this audience from you.

A community where members talk to each other — sharing prayer requests, life updates — is worth ~10× a page where only you post. Owning the audience isn't just safer; it's where ministry actually happens. MosesTab, "Church Communication Strategies"; SimpleTexting.

The depth ladder

Picture every viewer on a ladder. Your job in any single post is not to drag them to the top — it's to offer the one next rung. Asking for too big a leap gets ignored.

  1. Sees a clip rented — a stranger on the For You page
  2. Follows you rented — now in your orbit, but still the platform's to show
  3. Goes deeper on-platform rented — watches a full sermon, the podcast, a photo carousel
  4. Joins something you own owned — texts a keyword, joins the email list / group chat (the pivotal rung)
  5. Enters the online study owned — a recurring gathering, real names, real presence
  6. In-person / discipleship owned — Mobile, or a group near them

The rule: one CTA per post

Multiple asks split attention and people freeze — "comment, and follow, and check my bio, and share" nets you nothing. Pick one conversion goal per post, matched to where that viewer likely is on the ladder.

To grow reach (rented)

"Send this to someone who needs it." DM shares are the #1 algorithm signal — this beats "like" CTAs for spreading.

"Follow for Part 2" — promise a continuation.

To capture (owned) — higher value

"Comment the word HOME and I'll send you the full teaching." Keyword-in-comments + a DM converts 5–15% vs. ~1–3% for "link in bio."

"Text JOIN to ___" to get the weekly family devotional.

Dual delivery (non-negotiable): say the CTA aloud in the final 2–3 seconds and put it on screen as bold text. Most people watch muted; an unspoken-or-unwritten CTA is half-missed.
  • ❌ "Anyway, hope that helped, see you next time."
  • ✅ Spoken + on-screen: "Comment HOME — I'll send you the full message."
