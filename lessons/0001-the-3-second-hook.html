The 3-Second Hook

Write an opening line that stops the scroll — so your message of oneness and unbreakable families actually gets seen.

⏱ ~12 minutes🎯 One skill: draft a scroll-stopping hook✅ Win: 5 real hooks, scored

Here is the entire job of a hook: earn the next three seconds. Not explain. Not convert. Not summarize the sermon. Just buy three more seconds — then those three buy the next, until your message lands. That's it. If you believe nothing else from this lesson, believe that the opening line is a door, not a summary.

Clips that hold more than 65% of viewers past second 3 get 4–7× more reach than clips that don't. The algorithm rewards the hold, not your follower count — which is exactly why a brand-new account can reach thousands. OpusClip, "TikTok Hook Formulas That Drive 3-Second Holds."

The mental model: pull a trigger

A scroll is the brain on autopilot. A hook works by yanking one of four psychological levers that break autopilot. You don't need all four. Pull one well; stack two and you're dangerous.

Curiosity gapOpen a loop the brain must close. "Nobody tells you…"
Pattern interruptContradict the expected. "Love isn't the foundation — oneness is."
Self-relevanceName the exact person. "If you grew up in a divided home…"
Emotion / lossA real stake. We feel a loss ~2× a gain. "Your kids won't remember the gifts."

Full menu of fill-in-the-blank formulas lives in your Hook Library.

The 5-point check (memorize this)

  1. Short — ~12 words or fewer; ~2.5 seconds to say aloud.
  2. Pulls a trigger — at least one of the four.
  3. Specific — "the dinner table," not "spending time together."
  4. True to the clip — the video must deliver what the hook promises. No bait.
  5. Works on mute — said aloud and on screen as text in the first 2s (60%+ watch with sound off).

Watch one line become three hooks

Say your sermon contains this line: "A family finds its strength when everyone is pulling toward the same center." Flat as a hook. Now we run it through three triggers:

Raw line → "A family finds its strength when everyone pulls toward the same center."

  1. Curiosity: "Nobody tells you what actually holds a family together."
  2. Pattern interrupt: "A strong family isn't 50/50. It's 100/100."
  3. Self-relevance + loss: "If your home feels divided, you're missing one thing."
