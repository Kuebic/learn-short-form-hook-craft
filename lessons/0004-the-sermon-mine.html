The Sermon Mine

Turn one sermon you already have into a whole week of short clips — a repeatable system a two-person team can actually keep.

⏱ ~14 minutes🎯 One skill: mine + batch a week of content from one sermon✅ Win: a filled weekly calendar

The biggest reason small ministries quit online isn't bad content — it's the blank page, every single day. The fix is to stop creating daily and start mining: record once, then harvest many clips from that one recording. You're not a content factory. You're a mine with a rich seam already in the ground.

A single 30–45 minute sermon typically yields 8–15 short clips — enough to fuel an entire week (or more) of TikToks, Reels, and Shorts from one Sunday recording. Subsplash; Choppity; OpusClip sermon-clip data.

The 6 seams: where clips hide in a sermon

Don't re-watch hunting randomly. Listen for these six moment-types — each is a natural standalone clip:

💎 The quotable lineA sentence that stands alone and stings or sings.
📖 The scripture unlockA verse explained in a fresh, clarifying way.
🛠 The practical application"Here's what to do Monday morning."
❤️ The emotional peakThe moment the room goes quiet or tears up.
📕 The story payoffThe turn or punchline of an illustration.
🙋 The challenge / inviteA direct call that makes someone reflect.

Each clip still needs a hook (Lesson 0001) and one CTA (Lesson 0002). The mine gives you the raw ore; those lessons cut and polish it.

The weekly workflow (record once, post all week)

  1. Capture clean audio. The whole pipeline rests on this — clear audio = accurate transcripts and captions.
  2. Get a transcript. Auto-transcribe the sermon (most clip tools do this free). Now you can read for the 6 seams instead of re-watching.
  3. Mark the moments. Highlight 8–15 passages in the transcript. Tools like OpusClip / Choppity / Subsplash cut the video to match.
  4. Add hook + captions + one CTA. Top text hook, on-screen captions (60%+ watch muted), and a single call to action.
  5. Batch & schedule. Do this in one sitting for the week. Queue posts so daily-you just shows up to reply to comments.

Cadence: pick what you can keep for 30 days

Consistency beats frequency. Burnout is the real enemy — a pace you can hold for a month outperforms a heroic week followed by silence.

Common sustainable starting points: TikTok & Reels 3–5/week, YouTube Shorts 1–3/week. Batch 1–4 weeks of content in a single focused session. The Creator Insider; AIR Media-Tech; influence-flow cadence guides.
